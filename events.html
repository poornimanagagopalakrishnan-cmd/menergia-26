TECH EVENTS

PAPER PRESENTATION

Rules and Regulations

  • Participants can present individually or in a team of maximum 3 members.
  • The paper must be related to recent technologies or academic topics.
  • Participants must submit the abstract before the deadline.
  • The presentation time is limited to 8-10 minutes, followed by 2 minutes for questions.
  • PowerPoint presentation (PPT) is mandatory.
  • The PPT should contain maximum 10-12 slides.
  • Participants must bring their presentation in a pen drive and also keep a backup.
  • Papers should be original and plagiarism-free. Judges' decision will be final.
  • Participants should report at least 30 minutes before the event starts.
  • Formal dress code must be followed.
  • Any misconduct or violation of rules will lead to disqualification.

TECHNICAL QUIZ

Rules and Regulations

Maximum Members per Team: 3 students

  • Round 1: Preliminary Round (Screening Round)
    • Type: Written MCQ Round
    • Number of Questions: 30 Questions
    • Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions
    • Time Duration: 20 Minutes
    • Marking Scheme: 1 mark for each correct answer
    • Negative Mark: No negative marks
    • Top 10 teams with highest scores will qualify for the next round.
  • Round 2: Visual / Identification Round
    • Teams Participating: Top 10 Teams
    • Number of Questions: 10 questions per team
    • Question Type: Identify machine parts, Identify engineering tools, Identify circuits / diagrams
    • Time to Answer: 20 seconds
    • Marks: 10 marks per correct answer
  • Round 3: Rapid Fire Round / Buzzer Round
    • Teams Participating: Top 4 Teams
    • No passing allowed
    • Mode: Buzzer based
    • Time to Answer: 10 seconds

ROBO RUSH

Rules and Regulations

  • Each team can have maximum 3 members.
  • The robot must be manually controlled (wired or wireless).
  • The robot should fit within 15 cm × 15 cm × 15 cm dimensions at the start of the race.
  • The maximum weight of the robot should be 3 kg.
  • The robot must run on the given track without damaging the arena.
  • Participants must bring their own power supply, batteries, and controllers.
  • If the robot gets stuck, the team can restart from the previous checkpoint with penalty time.
  • Touching the robot during the race is not allowed unless permitted by the judges.
  • Any intentional damage to the track or other robots will lead to disqualification.
  • The team completing the track in the least time wins.
  • Participants should report to the event venue at least 30 minutes before the event.
  • Judges' decision will be final and binding.

NON-TECH EVENTS

SHORTFILM MAKING

Rules and Regulations

  • The competition is open to all college students.
  • Each team should have minimum 3 members and maximum 10 members.
  • The duration of the short film must be between 3 to 6 minutes.
  • The film can be in any language, but subtitles in English are recommended.
  • The theme of the short film should be social awareness / creativity / innovation (or as given by the organizers).
  • Obscene, offensive, or copyrighted content is strictly prohibited.
  • The short film must be original work created by the participants.
  • Films should be submitted in MP4 format with good video and audio quality.
  • Each team can submit only one entry.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
  • The organizing committee has the right to disqualify any film that violates the rules.
  • Films must be submitted before the specified deadline.

AD ZAP

Rules and Regulations

  • Each team should consist of minimum 2 and maximum 5 members.
  • Participants will be given a product or concept on the spot.
  • Teams will get 10 minutes preparation time before performing.
  • The performance time should not exceed 5 minutes.
  • Participants can use simple props if required, but they must bring them on their own.
  • Use of vulgar, offensive, or inappropriate content is strictly prohibited.
  • The advertisement should be creative, humorous, and relevant to the given product.
  • Language can be English or Tamil.
  • Judges' decision will be final and binding.
  • Any team exceeding the time limit may lose marks.
  • Participants should report to the event venue 30 minutes before the start.
  • Discipline must be maintained throughout the event.

WATER ROCKETERY

Rules and Regulations

Team Size: Maximum 3 members per team

  • Rockets must be assembled before attending the event.
  • Each team must have only one rocket.
  • Only cardboard can be used for the wings.
  • A single 1-litre water bottle must be used as the pressure vessel.
  • The only permissible sources of propulsion are water and air pressure.
  • The pressure must not exceed 45 psi.
  • Trial launch is available (optional).
  • In case of a tie, another launch will be conducted.
  • Use of metal, glass, hard plastics, spikes, or antennas is strictly prohibited for safety reasons.
  • If a rocket is deemed harmful, unstable, or dangerous, the team will be disqualified.
  • Only one attempt will be allowed for the launch.
  • Only the official launch will be evaluated.
